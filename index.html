VOLCANIC ERUPTION

Volcanic eruptions may be subtle or explosive and can produce dangerous lava flows, poisonous gases, and flying rocks and ash. If you live near a volcano active or dormant be prepared to act on short notice.

Before

Be aware and be alert for the announcement given by the government. Stay tuned to local official channels.

Secure items: Put all machines and animals in a safe area. Secure loose things that ash could blow away.

Evacuation plan: Prepare an evacuation plan, know how and where you would go if you are told to evacuate.

Disaster kit: Assemble a "go bag" with emergency supplies like water, food, flashlight, masks, first-aid, important docs.

Safety at home: Check that structural items are safe and that you can move quickly if ordered to evacuate.

During

Wear protective clothing: Long-sleeved shirts and pants to reduce skin exposure to ash and debris.

Respiratory protection: Wear safety goggles and a mask to avoid breathing in ash.

Shelter inside: Close all doors and windows to keep ash from entering buildings and switch off ventilation if necessary.

Follow orders: Follow evacuation orders from authorities immediately. Move to higher ground if advised.

Avoid danger: Do not go near the volcano or river valleys that could carry lahars (mudflows).

After: Stay safe & check

At home: When it is safe to return, check your house, roofs and structures for damage before entering. Use masks during cleaning.

Await announcements

Wait for announcements and official clearance from authorities before resuming normal activities as roads, utilities, and services may still be unsafe.

Check & help

Before going home, check that family members are safe and that basic services (water, electricity) are safe to use. Help neighbors if you can. If you experience any symptoms like difficulty in breathing or persistent coughing, seek medical help.

STAY SAFE AND AWARE
